# WAEC/JAMB Subject Weakness Audit Worksheet

This worksheet helps you stop guessing and identify what to study first. You should use it before creating a timetable, before paying for extra lessons, and before asking AI to plan your revision. If you do not know your weak topics, your timetable will be based on feelings instead of evidence.

## Rule

Do not study only what you enjoy. Study what the exam is likely to test and what you currently do not understand.

## Why The Audit Matters

Many students say, "I am weak in Mathematics" or "I do not understand Chemistry." That statement may be true, but it is too broad to fix. Mathematics contains topics. Chemistry contains topics. English contains different skills. If you do not break the subject down, you may spend many hours reading without touching the exact area that is reducing your score.

The audit turns fear into information. When you see the topics clearly, you can decide what to do next. You may discover that you are not weak in the whole subject. You may only be weak in algebra, comprehension, organic chemistry, map reading, genetics, or essay structure. Once the weakness becomes specific, it becomes easier to attack.

You should not use the audit to shame yourself. Low scores are not insults. They are signals. A score of 0 means, "I have not dealt with this yet." A score of 1 means, "I need explanation and practice." A score of 2 means, "I need more questions." A score of 3 means, "I should maintain this topic and move attention elsewhere."

## How To Use AI After The Audit

After filling the worksheet, copy your weakest topics into AI and use this prompt:

> I am preparing for [EXAM]. These are my subjects and weak topics: [PASTE LIST]. Help me group them into urgent, important, and maintain. Then create a two-week study order. Include explanation time, practice questions, and mistake review.

Do not accept the first timetable blindly. Check whether it matches your school schedule and your energy. If the plan is too heavy, say:

> Reduce this plan to something realistic for a student who has [HOURS] per day and still attends school.

You can also ask AI to explain why one topic should come before another:

> Explain why I should study [TOPIC A] before [TOPIC B]. If the order does not matter, tell me.

This helps you understand the strategy instead of simply obeying a timetable.

## Priority Rules

Use these rules when choosing what to revise first:

- Start with topics that appear often in past questions.
- Start with topics that block other topics.
- Start with topics where a little improvement can raise your score quickly.
- Do not ignore compulsory subjects.
- Do not spend the whole week on only your favorite subject.
- Do not wait until the final month before touching difficult topics.

If two topics look equally important, choose the one you can begin today. Momentum matters.

## Weekly Audit Review

Repeat this audit every week. Your scores should change. If a topic remains at 0 or 1 for many weeks, you need a different approach. You may need a teacher, a classmate, a video lesson, a textbook explanation, or a slower AI-guided practice session.

At the end of each week, ask:

- Which score improved?
- Which score stayed the same?
- Which topic did I avoid?
- Which topic produced the most wrong answers?
- What is my first topic for next week?

This worksheet is not just a form. It is your study decision system.

## Example Of A Completed Audit Decision

Imagine you are preparing for English, Mathematics, Biology, Chemistry, and Government. You score English comprehension as 2, essay writing as 1, algebra as 1, statistics as 3, genetics as 0, organic chemistry as 1, and political parties as 2. Without an audit, you may simply say, "I need to read more." With the audit, your next actions become clearer.

You can decide that genetics needs a beginner explanation because the score is 0. Algebra and organic chemistry need guided practice because the score is 1. Statistics only needs maintenance because the score is 3. Essay writing needs section-by-section practice because it affects English marks directly.

This is how you avoid wasting time. You are no longer studying to feel busy. You are studying to close visible gaps.

## What To Do When Everything Looks Weak

Sometimes your audit may look discouraging because many topics are at 0 or 1. Do not panic. Choose the topics that can unlock other topics first. In Mathematics, basic algebra may help you with equations, graphs, word problems, and some science calculations. In English, comprehension and sentence structure may help you across many question types. In Biology, understanding cells may help you understand tissues, organs, genetics, and reproduction.

Ask AI:

> These are my weak topics: [PASTE LIST]. Which topics should I study first because they will help me understand other topics later?

Then start with only two or three priority topics for the week. You do not need to solve every weakness in one week. You need to build momentum and prove that improvement is possible.
