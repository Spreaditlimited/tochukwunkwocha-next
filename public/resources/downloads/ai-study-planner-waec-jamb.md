# 7-Day AI Study Planner for WAEC/JAMB Learners

Use this planner to turn AI into a study assistant, not a shortcut. The goal is simple: know what to study, practice deliberately, review mistakes, and build confidence without pretending that AI can replace your own effort.

## Before You Start

Write down:
- Your exam: WAEC, JAMB, NECO, or school mock.
- Your subjects.
- Your weakest three topics in each subject.
- Your available study time each day.
- Your exam or mock test date.

Then use this prompt:

> I am preparing for [EXAM]. My subjects are [SUBJECTS]. My weakest topics are [TOPICS]. I have [HOURS] each day for study. Help me create a realistic 7-day revision plan with practice questions, review time, and rest breaks. Do not overload the timetable.

## Day 1: Map the Exam

Your first job is to stop studying randomly.

Tasks:
- List all subjects.
- Ask AI to break each subject into major topics.
- Mark each topic as Strong, Average, or Weak.
- Pick the three weakest topics to start with.

Prompt:

> Act as a WAEC/JAMB study planner. Break [SUBJECT] into major exam topics. Help me classify them as Strong, Average, or Weak based on my self-assessment. Then suggest the first three topics I should revise.

## Day 2: Build a Realistic Timetable

Do not create a timetable you cannot follow. A good timetable respects school, chores, church/mosque, family responsibilities, and rest.

Tasks:
- Create 25-50 minute study blocks.
- Put harder subjects when your energy is high.
- Add short review blocks.
- Add one daily past-question practice block.

Prompt:

> Create a study timetable for me. I can study from [TIME] to [TIME]. My hardest subjects are [SUBJECTS]. Build a timetable with short breaks, practice questions, and revision review.

## Day 3: Learn Weak Topics Properly

Use AI like a patient tutor.

Tasks:
- Pick one weak topic.
- Ask AI for a simple explanation.
- Ask for examples.
- Ask for 5 practice questions.
- Answer before checking.

Prompt:

> Teach me [TOPIC] in [SUBJECT] like I am preparing for WAEC/JAMB. Explain it simply, give examples, then ask me 5 practice questions one by one. Wait for my answer before marking.

## Day 4: Review Mistakes

Mistakes are not failure. They show where learning is needed.

Tasks:
- Collect wrong answers from practice.
- Ask AI to explain why each answer is wrong.
- Write the correct method in your notebook.
- Create one revision flashcard per mistake.

Prompt:

> I got these questions wrong: [PASTE QUESTIONS AND ANSWERS]. Explain why my answers are wrong, show the correct reasoning, and give me a short rule I can remember.

## Day 5: Timed Practice

Exams test knowledge and speed. Practice under time.

Tasks:
- Set a timer.
- Answer without checking notes.
- Mark after the timer ends.
- Record your score and weak areas.

Prompt:

> Create a timed WAEC/JAMB-style quiz for [SUBJECT] on [TOPIC]. Give me [NUMBER] questions. Do not show answers until I submit my answers.

## Day 6: Teach It Back

If you can explain a topic clearly, you understand it better.

Tasks:
- Explain one topic in your own words.
- Ask AI to check your explanation.
- Rewrite the explanation.
- Teach a friend, sibling, or parent.

Prompt:

> Here is my explanation of [TOPIC]: [YOUR EXPLANATION]. Check it like a teacher. Tell me what is correct, what is missing, and how to improve it.

## Day 7: Final Review and Confidence Check

Do not panic. Use the final day to consolidate.

Tasks:
- Do a mixed quiz.
- Review only weak topics.
- Stop late-night cramming.
- Sleep properly.

Prompt:

> Give me a final mixed revision quiz for [SUBJECT]. Focus on my weak topics: [TOPICS]. After marking, tell me the top 3 things I should revise again.

## Safety Note

Do not use AI to cheat, copy assignments, or avoid thinking. Use it to understand, practice, and improve.

## How To Make The Planner Work In Real Life

A study planner only works when it is honest. Do not write a perfect timetable that looks impressive but ignores your real life. If you return from school tired, your first study block should not be your hardest subject. If you know you have chores at a particular time, do not pretend that time is available. If you use a shared phone or have limited data, plan your AI sessions for the times when the device and internet are available.

Start small enough to continue. A consistent 45-minute focused session is better than a five-hour plan you abandon after two days. If you are preparing for WAEC and JAMB at the same time, do not try to revise all subjects every day. Use rotation. Choose priority subjects for the week and keep other subjects warm with shorter review blocks.

Your planner should always contain three types of work:

- Understanding work: this is where you ask AI to explain a topic, give examples, and help you understand the idea.
- Practice work: this is where you answer questions without looking at notes or asking AI for the answer first.
- Correction work: this is where you review mistakes, write down what went wrong, and practice similar questions again.

Many students do understanding work and stop there. That feels comfortable because reading explanations creates the feeling of progress. Exams, however, reward recall, application, and speed. You must practice. You must also correct. A wrong answer that you understand properly can become one of your strongest future topics.

## Daily Study Session Template

Use this structure for any subject:

1. Choose one topic for the session.
2. Write what you already know about the topic in two or three lines.
3. Ask AI to explain the topic simply and give examples.
4. Close the explanation and answer practice questions.
5. Mark the questions.
6. Paste only the wrong or confusing questions into AI and ask for correction.
7. Write one rule, formula, definition, or warning you must remember.
8. Schedule a short re-practice session within three days.

Here is a useful prompt:

> I am studying [TOPIC] in [SUBJECT]. First, ask me what I already know. Then explain only the parts I do not understand. After that, give me 5 practice questions without answers. Wait for my answers before marking.

## Weekly Review

At the end of the week, do not only ask, "Did I read?" Ask better questions:

- Which topic became clearer?
- Which topic is still weak?
- Which mistake repeated more than once?
- Which subject did I avoid?
- Which study time worked best?
- What should change next week?

Paste your weekly tracking table into AI and ask:

> Review my study week. Tell me what improved, what I avoided, what mistake pattern you can see, and how I should plan next week.

## Parent or Guardian Check-In

If a parent or guardian is supporting you, show them your tracker once a week. They do not need to understand every subject. They can still help you stay accountable by asking what you practiced, what score changed, and what you will revise next.

The best support is calm and specific. Instead of waiting until results come out, use this planner to make your preparation visible every week.
